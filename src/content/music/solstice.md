Солнцеворот is an album made by the Russian band Гражданская оборона (Grazhdanskaya Oborona) in 1995 and remixed in 2005. 

I think fist listened to [Родина](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/5r4aNBDVKdLa4erSi2rLXX?si=77bd701bec3f4fc0) (Rodina // Motherland) in an Instagram reel (God bless reels) on a trip back to Spain for Christmas, and I found their sound amusing. I would say they sound like you bought Dostoevsky to the 90s, taught him how to play and put efects on a guitar, and recorded him. Apparently, since I don't know Russian, this song talks about the motherland in a mocking way. 

![You found me in a very Russian time](/blog/images/you_found_me.jpg)
>This is how I felt when I discovered this album

After listening to that song a few times during the trip, I was like "I need more of this shit", so I started listening to the album. It's funny because this album is so good as a whole that I only liked 3 songs on Spotify, since I always listen to the album itself, rather than the individual songs.

In my opinion, the best song of the album is [Про дурачка](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/14WsvC38qgF6NDNuy84Z05?si=6cb3b8574a394336) (Pro durachka // About a Fool). The atmosphere of this song is just unique. I don't think I've ever listened to anything quite like this before. The thousand shoegazy guitar layers, the guy's voice, probably talking about how miserable life is, or some poetical Russian stuff, and ESPECIALLY, the female (apparently the singer's then-wife) voice in the background, which sounds angelical, make this song a big chef's kiss. A few days ago, I was listening to it while being high and, I swear I touched the sky, came back, and I went back to 1995 and hung out with the band in the studio for a while. 10/10 experience.

I don't have the musical knowledge to do a proper analysis of this album, but I can give you some basic info. In terms of the sound, we can see a clear inspiration from the already mentioned "[Shoegaze](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shoegaze)" genre, where a lot of guitar layers are used to create a "Wall of Sound". For example, in [Про дурачка](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/14WsvC38qgF6NDNuy84Z05?si=6cb3b8574a394336), we can find (at least) the following instruments (they are order based on the moment they appear):

1. Drums (duh)
2. Bass doing root notes (boring ahh bass but we are playing alt, not funk)
3. Lead guitar doing the main riff (panned to the left)
4. Organ (why not)
5. Another guitar harmonizing the main melody (panned to the right)
6. Main voice
7. Another guitar doing arpeggios in the high strings (not fully sure, could be the organ again)
8. Backing vocals 1 (doing half of the melody, panned on the right)
9. Backing vocals 2 (doing the other half, panned on the left) (Thinking about it, dividing the backing voice melody into two, and panning them on opposite sides is what creates that "angelical" feeling, like you had two angels singing to your sides.)
10. Lead guitar doing a solo (probably not a different instrument, just different melody) (on the spotify version it's the organ who does the solo)

This big army of instruments definitely helps create that rich atmosphere shoegaze is known for, even though this is not really shoegaze. The thing I love about this album is that it's the opposite side of the same coin. I feel that Solntsevorot is like trying to make [Loveless](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loveless_(album)) but from the other side of the iron curtain. They are definitely trying to make something that sounds like [My Bloody Valentine](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/artist/3G3Gdm0ZRAOxLrbyjfhii5?si=LArr9o17RwiBEuEc5B0RMw) or [Jesus and Mary Chain](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/artist/4rjlerN21ygkIhmUv55irs?si=dwm4nLLoS8iYxgseryjkAA), but you can feel the russian background. The melodies have a military/patriotic tint, probably inherited from socialist realism, and the lyrics (or the voice sound at least, since I don't understand the lyrics) sound like the pessimistic poetical literature Russia is known for.
