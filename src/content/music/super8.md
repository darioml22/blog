Another 90s Spanish Pop-rock album yayyyyy. Los Planetas have been MY band for the last year. I think the first time I listened to them was trough the weekly discover on Spotify. I heared [Pesadilla En El Parque de Atracciones](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/1dU5WwKslqch2IkJWtbYcH?si=94a63627c52648c8) back in July 2024, but it would be one year later (around January 2025) that I actually started listening to them. I started with songs from their first album, Super 8, and sudenly I have 3 vinyls, one book and a poster of them. 

![My stuff from Los Planetas](/blog/images/LosPlanetasStuff.jpeg)

This album is highly influenced by the alternative music sounding in the UK at the time (early 90s), shoegaze, noise pop, noise rock, etc... Guitars with enough distorsion and reverb to stop a train, simple basslines and amazing drum fills. Even tough they always stay inside the noise pop sound, their songs have a lot of variation. We have the Pop Punk sound of [Jesus](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/62QXEdYZw6nZekI6hkPpEW?si=ccc3deaed94b496a), a more easy-to-listen pop in [Que puedo hacer](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/6cTzdFQDV7B3Au4TJHSiRo?si=38b007ea385b4cd0), a more relaxed vibe in [Estos Ultimos Dias](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/4Yx0VGaFpeAVyPrRdifeJA?si=7012e7303e544104), or a "Why not" 10 minutes song talking about how he has a devil in a box [La Caja Del Diablo](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/3WTgg65LmPNVXN3eyVdX8P?si=80fb4e6b0ab74bc7). 

I would personally highlight:
1. The ending of "Estos Ultimos Dias" where Florent, the guitarrist starts playing one of the most beautiful solos ever in my opinion
2. "La caja del diablo". The lyrics are complete madness, and it just sounds so good
3. "De viaje" and "Brigitte" because of their lyrics

I think this album (and Los Planetas as a whole) captivated me for exactly the same reason they captivated alternative spanish youth in the 90s and 00s, the deep atmosphere they create with the guitars, and the beauty and simplicity of Jota's lyrics. It is true that Los Planetas, especially in their first albums, run into a lot of cliches, but I feel that Jota doesn't prettend to hide it. Instead of giving some abstract and ambiguos quote about love, Jota says it directly, which makes it very easy to feel relatable to his lyrics.

If this guys caugh you attention, I really suggest you to watch [Segundo Premio](https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Segundo_premio_(pel%C3%ADcula)), a movie about them recoring their 3rd studio album "Una Semana En El Motor De Un Autobús // A Week In The Engine Of A Bus". 

They play with music, drugs, and relationships. It's an amazing movie in my opinion. If by any chance you are part of the 92% of the world population who doesn't speak spanish, I ensure you there are subtitles out there. And if you watched it, hit me up so we can talk about the movie!!!
