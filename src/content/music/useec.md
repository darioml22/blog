Un Soplo En El Corazon (A breath in the heart) is a indie pop/synth-pop album made by Family, a very misterious band from San Sebastián, in the north of Spain, and released in 1994. This is "the favourite rapper of your favourity rapper" type music. Family, and this album, are a bit unknown (24k monthly listeners in Spotify), but anyone that has dig down a bit in spanish pop will probably know them.  

The main reason for this it's probably the little information available about the band. They only released one studio album, together with some other demos, and dissapeared from the face of earth. They only appeared in a few concerts, around the time the album was released, there is only two pictures of the band members together, they gave no interviews and one of them is now a painter.

Even though they are kinda unknown, they were (and are) highly influential in the spanish pop scene. To this day, two tribute albums have been released, where other bands do covers of the songs of Un Soplo En El Corazon. Los Planetas (one of the biggest alternative bands in Spain in the 90s) have mentioned Family as an inspiration, and famous singer Alaska was friends with Javier Aramburu (Singer of Family, the painter I mentioned before). Javier (who used to be a graphic designer) is the mind behind a lot of important covers from the 90s in Spain, for bands like Los Planetas, Le Mans, La Buena Vida, Fuerza Nueva, and, of course, Family.

This song is "Martín Se Ha Ido Para Siempre" (Martín Left For Good). It's the last song of the album, in my opinion a masterpiece and probably my favourite song at the moment. With a highly synth-pop sound, a synth bass and a drum machine, probably inspired by New Order (I even saw some people online saying that this song is copying "Bizarre Love Triangle") this song makes sadness danceable. The song talks about the last moments of a couple, about how "El calor que antes encontraban, ya no esta en su habitación // The heat they used to feel, is not on the room anymore", but they still care about the loved one "Abrígate // Bundle up" (As in, "it's cold out there, take care")

**10/10 album, must listen**
