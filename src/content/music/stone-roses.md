Today we are moving to the UK, to the late 80s. [The Stone Roses](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/album/0um9FI6BLBldL5POP4D4Cw?si=kISVy_-fRxGfE92lZlyl7g) is a self-titled album released in 1989. (Before checking the date, I could swear that the album had been released around 1994, but apparently it's way older.) It could be categorized as alternative/indie rock or Madchester.

### Influences

Something I love about this album is the huge amount of inspiration it draws from. I would say The Stone Roses is where The Beatles meet The Jesus and Mary Chain, with a light touch of Manchester rave culture from the 90s.

The main influences of this album are:

1. [**The Beatles.**](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/artist/3WrFJ7ztbogyGnTHbHJFl2?si=L0hTxTFmRzqyGcW_xYr5oQ) You can feel it in their clean melodies and guitars.
2. [**Jimi Hendrix.**](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/artist/776Uo845nYHJpNaStv1Ds4?si=sskUJNxaSzSUE2xIiJC5ew) The guitar solos are inspired by him.
3. [**The Smiths.**](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/artist/3yY2gUcIsjMr8hjo51PoJ8?si=pdAuds--RqSk1UyzEkB_5g) The jangle guitar style from bands like The Smiths can be felt throughout this album.
4. [**The Jesus and Mary Chain.**](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/artist/4rjlerN21ygkIhmUv55irs?si=RHMYGLGbRcOSSEabZbu31g) The taste for controlled distortion on the guitars, which would lead to Dream Pop and Shoegaze years later.
5. **Acid house.** It can be felt in some songs, like "Fools Gold" or "Shoot You Down", with some breakbeat drum patterns.

### Songs

Moving into the songs, the album is opened by the wonderful ["I Wanna Be Adored"](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/758wEKVqfYopJIHYWdLVd4?si=093a74dd75f1473e) (the first song by the band I listened to, and an absolute banger). With very simple lyrics, a repetitive bassline, and very dreamy guitars, this song feels like a trance, giving it a psychedelic and rave-ish vibe.

> I wanna be adored

This is 70% of the song.

Continuing with the songs, ["She Bangs the Drums"](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/3clIHVzJ9J2rvvrFUKLICo?si=e22fe9c1ff394364) definitely has some of that The Smiths inspiration. The lyrics also follow that influence, being about love but in a more ambiguous way. When I listen to songs, I like trying to figure out what instrument came up with the original idea. I feel that both this song and "I Wanna Be Adored" were originally bassline ideas, and the band built the songs around them.

In my opinion, in ["Waterfall"](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/4CWgqHRJjDLd2PCedMz8eY?si=d49404f5dae84eb8), you can feel a bit of the The Jesus and Mary Chain influence. The lyrics are (just a bit) buried in the mix, and they are more like a whisper. The guitars are not as dirty, but you can still feel the similarities. The end of the song is pure 60s psychedelia, Beatles-type stuff.

["Don't Stop"](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/2Kwf8mUygPEriB7GVErizM?si=8a28d0adc2784a51) follows the psychedelic path from the previous song, with reverse delay, sudden stops, and ambiguous lyrics.

["Bye Bye Bad Man"](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/2r0Cx2vW1wn2K3PogcdcPX?si=60125d7e27f447c1) changes the tone a bit. This is a more "traditionally structured" song, with an incredibly beautiful pre-chorus: not one, but two guitars doing arpeggios besides the main rhythm guitar, a very immersive bassline, and a beautiful vocal melody. Then, in the chorus, we jump straight into a folk sound, with the countertempo drums. The end of the song reminds me of the Spanish pop band ["Mirafiori"](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/artist/6xSmMSRGkSQSWYcmU87vwG?si=yOg7J3lAR3KUrW8_ZW4AQQ) (I will review them one day). They have nothing to do with each other, but they sound similar to me :)

["Elizabeth My Dear"](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/395XURgt6hWMx9XNOfIxia?si=8c6cae77a9d84e99), ["(Song for My) Sugar Spun Sister"](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/3WEDBjYdFN9anBLB87w9b8?si=87ab63b2cf6d4bfd), and ["Made of Stone"](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/0bZelokqNQicLQfLU1Ayw8?si=ee6a913bcee84d31) are a bit more of the same. The same goes for ["Shoot You Down"](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/2erQ477GekpdP5l3xp5adl?si=b8b4fb6daf4c4ba1), but in this case, as I said before, we can start to see the breakbeat influence in the drums.

Now, going back to the bangers, ["This Is the One"](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/433EQGQOsQjWvD5eImXkHf?si=0379cef5df7f4c08) starts, like "I Wanna Be Adored", with a hypnotic bassline. Guitar, drums, and vocals join later. Like in the other songs, Ian Brown likes to be very ambiguous about the lyrics, so I'm not going to analyse them too much. He probably talks about trying to flee his routine and start a new life, but who knows. The chorus is very beautiful though.

> I'd like to leave the country
> Burn the town where I was born

["I Am the Resurrection"](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/5ikdUUm6JbnEVnp35c7dvy?si=e8d39d6075854119) is an interesting song. It follows the same path we had throughout the whole album, with yet another incredible chorus full of reverb and guitars, but halfway through the song they jump into a breakbeat section (fuck yeah, why not).

["Fools Gold"](https://open.spotify.com/intl-es/track/2mv6GY70UsHiTCGQZ4JfgC?si=0efdc4e1019f468e) closes the album with a whole jungle track, embracing the rave culture of the time.

### Conclusion

In general, I would say this is a timeless album, with a lot of different influences. You can feel The Beatles, but you can also see the origins of the 90s alternative scene that was born in the UK and would later be exported to the rest of the world.
